Pre-PrimarySchool

Pre-primary school usually serves children between the ages of three and five, preparing them for formal schooling by providing a play-based learning environment that promotes their physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development. The goal of pre-primary school is to provide children with the foundation they need to succeed academically and socially in primary school and beyond.

PrimarySchool

Primary school, also known as elementary school, is an educational institution that provides formal education to children between the ages of 5-11, typically from grades K-5 or K-6. The primary school curriculum usually includes subjects such as mathematics, science, English language arts, social studies, physical education, and art/music.
The primary goal of primary school is to provide children with a strong foundation in fundamental academic skills such as reading, writing, and arithmetic, as well as social and emotional development

MiddleSchool

Middle school, also known as junior high school, is an educational institution that provides formal education to children between the ages of 11-14, typically from grades 6-8 or 7-9.
The middle school curriculum usually builds on the foundational skills and knowledge gained in primary school and includes subjects such as mathematics, science, English language arts, social studies, foreign languages, physical education, and elective courses such as art, music, or technology.
The goal of middle school is to provide students with a comprehensive education that prepares them for high school and beyond. In addition to academic skills, middle school students also develop social and emotional skills, such as teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving.
Middle school education is often seen as a crucial transition period for students as they navigate the challenges of adolescence and prepare for the academic and social demands of high school.

HighSchool

High school is an educational institution that provides formal education to students between the ages of 14-18, typically from grades 9-10. High school is also referred to as secondary school, senior high school, or college preparatory school.
The high school curriculum builds on the foundational knowledge and skills acquired in middle school and includes a range of core academic subjects such as mathematics, science, English language arts, social studies, foreign languages, and electives such as arts, music, or physical education.
High school education also emphasizes the development of critical thinking, problem-solving, and research skills that are necessary for success in higher education and the workforce. Additionally, high school education provides students with opportunities to explore their interests and passions through extracurricular activities, clubs, and organizations.
The completion of high school education is often seen as a significant milestone in a student's life, and it is a requirement for entry into many post-secondary educational programs and careers.
